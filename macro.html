Today's Analysis

Geopolitical Crisis Overshadows Macro — S&P 500 Down 1.56%, 10Y Yields Spike

Risk-Off Rotation. The S&P 500 dropped 1.56% to 5,680 as equities sell off on Iran tensions. Energy, Defense, and Industrials are leading—beneficiaries of the oil shock. Consumer discretionary and tech are bleeding. This is a classic rotation into hard assets and away from growth narratives.

Dollar Weakness. The DXY slipped 0.27% to 99.20. Surprisingly weak given the typical risk-off dollar bid. Explanation: Oil priced in dollars is spiking, so importers need fewer dollars to hedge. The rise in oil is actually dollar-negative when crude is the shock driver.

10Y Yields Push Higher. The 10-year Treasury yield rose to 4.35% (+0.07) as inflation expectations reset upward. Oil at $98 and natural gas at $4.85 mean stagflationary pressure is real. The Fed cannot cut rates into this.

Implied Inflation Shock. Breakeven inflation rates (TIPS spreads) are repricing higher. The market is now pricing in a 3%+ inflation print for the next 12 months. This undermines the Fed’s rate-cut narrative that was building just days ago.

Economic Calendar — CPI Tomorrow is Everything
▶ This Week's Key Events

TOMORROW, March 11: US CPI (YoY forecast 2.2%) — Core CPI (forecast 0.2% MoM) — This is THE number. If headline comes in hot, oil shock gets blamed. If core stays cool, transitory relief. But if BOTH accelerate, the Fed is cornered: stagflation.

March 14: Retail Sales — Consumer spending test amid higher gas/energy costs

Watch List: Oil price stabilization updates. Strait of Hormuz status. Any escalation = new CPI upside risk.

Fed Policy Outlook — Rate Cuts on Hold?
▶ Rate Expectations & Stagflation Risk

Rate Cut Narrative Reversed: Just 48 hours ago, markets priced 2-3 cuts by year-end. Today, with oil at $98 and natural gas spiking, that narrative is dead. Fed funds futures now price a 50/50 chance of NO cuts in 2026 if geopolitical tension persists.

Powell’s Dilemma: The Fed cannot cut rates into a stagflationary shock. Higher oil = higher inflation. Higher inflation = can't cut. But a risk-off market punishes the Fed for staying patient. Powell will hold steady and blame exogenous factors (Iran/Strait).

Base Case: Fed on pause through mid-2026. Cuts only if geopolitical resolves AND inflation falls. Upside: inflation stays sticky, no cuts all year.
