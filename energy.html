Today's Analysis

Oil Explodes as Strait of Hormuz Closes — Brent Touches $120

Iran Crisis Unleashed. WTI exploded +47.6% to $98.50 and Brent spiked +71.3% to $120.00 on Iran tensions and the closure of the Strait of Hormuz. Through that chokepoint flows 20%–25% of the world’s seaborne oil. This is a genuine supply shock in motion.

Supply Disruption Real. Tankers are rerouting, insurers are repricing risk, and strategic reserves are being assessed. If the closure lasts more than days, we could see structural shortages and $120+ WTI sustained. Brent already touching $120 signals the market is pricing in a severe scenario.

Natural Gas Up 13.3%. Nat Gas surged to $4.85 as LNG export terminals and regional infrastructure face elevated geopolitical risk. Heating season tail risk + supply concerns = another leg higher.

Refiner Margins Explode. Crack spreads (WTI-product spreads) are blowing out. Downstream players face margin compression while upstream producers are thrilled. OPEC is in no rush to increase production in this environment.

Strait of Hormuz Crisis — Supply Shock Scenario
▶ Geopolitical Supply Risk

Strait Closure Impact: A 3–7 day closure = massive supply shock. 15–20 million bbl/day normally transits daily. Immediate spot prices have spiked to $98 WTI/$120 Brent. Extended closure (14+ days) could push WTI toward $120–$150.

OPEC Response: Unlikely to increase production during chaos. Rather, OPEC is collecting windfall profits. The group will wait for geopolitical stabilization before easing supply.

Market Implications: Inflation shock. Airlines and shippers face immediate cost pressures. Global growth forecasts will be cut. This is stagflationary—and central banks cannot fight it with rate cuts.
